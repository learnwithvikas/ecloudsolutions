Cloud Computing - What, Why & How

When you are on the Cloud, the Sky is your limit!

Cloud Computing provides wonderful possibilities for everyone. From a hundred dollar startup to a billion dollar enterprise like Netflix, the cloud supports them all. Cloud computing provides for them all. The scalability, affordability and functionality that the cloud provides is too good for anyone to ignore.

In spite of all the wonderful benefits of moving into the cloud, there are several misconceptions that seem to hold back many businesses, preventing them from utilizing the wonders that are available on the cloud

Already on the Cloud!

This is the most common. In an hasty move to the cloud, people end up with a simple "Lift and Drop" approach. Simply purchasing server instances on the cloud, to deploy their application as is. This will surely reduce your maintenance effort, but may not result in real savings. You might even spend more than you did, if the configurations are not good.

The value of migrating to the clouds is not just in getting rid of your local infrastructures. We can get the real value when we understand the cloud services well. Then we can optimize our deployment and make it rightly scalable. And that is just the begining. If we can get a serverless deployment, there is no limit to the kind of savings and scalability that we can enjoy.

Security

"Data is insecure on the cloud". This is one of the most common misconception that people carry. But, the fact is, anything that is connected to the internet is equally vulnerable to hackers. If your data is locked into a hard disk, disconnected from any other digital media, the only risk you have is that of physical damage. Apart from that, anything connected to the internet is equally vulnerable to the hackers, who are always eager to break in. Your campus is as vulnerable as the cloud.

But, the point is, Amazon, Microsoft and Google understand security much better than we muggles do! Naturally, they can secure our data much better than anyone else. Your data on the cloud is far more secure than on your own campus - Just as your money in Citibank is a lot more secure than the cash burried in your godowns.

Vendor Locking

When the concept of cloud computing was new, people were not sure of how long it would work. So people did not want to trust Amazon with all that they had. People were nervous about - and planned for a day when the clouds collapse and they would have to come back to their campus. But that is not the problem today. Cloud Computing has survived the test of time. Yes, using the cloud to its fullest potential will mean getting locked down to the cloud vendor.

But that is not a business risk any more. We know for sure that Amazon or Microsoft or Google are not going anywhere! In fact, we are all locked down to Google for almost everything we do. Phones, mails, search, maps, and what not. One cannot immagine a life without Google. But that is not a problem. Because Google is not going anywhere. Google, Microsoft and Amazon are too big to disappear overnight. We are absolutely safe when we depend on them.

Data is the new Oil

We have often heard them say - Data is the new Oil. But, unlike the traditional oil, data is available everywhere, in abundance. Each of us is sitting on millions - only lacking the ability to dig down and identify and utilize the data that we already have. Most of our applications generate a huge amount of data - we don't even know about it.

Migrating to the cloud does not bring out the data on a platter. But that makes it a lot easier for us to identify an analyze such data. It is a lot easier to identify and leverage such data when our application is on the cloud. If the cloud deployment is done well, we can create a better visibility into the process and simplify the process of identifying and analyzing the useful data.
